üö® Urgency Guidelines
  • STAT (Same day): Acute vision loss, CRAO/CRVO, chemical burn, acute angle closure, endophthalmitis, retinal detachment symptoms
  • URGENT (24-48 hrs): IOP > 30, severe eye pain, corneal ulcer, orbital cellulitis, new onset diplopia, papilledema
  • SOON (1-2 weeks): IOP 24-30, moderate uveitis, diabetic macular edema, wet AMD with new symptoms
  • ROUTINE (4-6 weeks): Glaucoma suspect, stable cataracts, routine diabetic exam, dry AMD monitoring
üìÖ Appointment Duration Guidelines
  • New patient comprehensive: 60 minutes
  • New patient consultation: 45 minutes
  • Follow-up: 20-30 minutes
  • Post-op day 1: 15 minutes
  • Visual field only: 30 minutes
üìû When to Call the Doctor
  • Patient reports sudden vision loss (call immediately)
  • IOP reading > 30 mmHg on pretesting
  • Patient describes flashes AND new floaters together
  • Referral marked "urgent" or "emergent"
  • Prior auth denied - needs peer-to-peer
  • Patient in significant distress or appears unwell
üìã Common Workup Protocols
  • Glaucoma Suspect: IOP, pachymetry, gonioscopy, OCT RNFL, HVF 24-2
  • Diabetic Eye Exam: Dilated fundus exam, OCT macula, fundus photos
  • Cataract Evaluation: BCVA, refraction, IOL calculations, OCT macula
  • Macular Degeneration: OCT macula, fundus photos, Amsler grid
  • Dry Eye: TBUT, Schirmer's, corneal staining, meibography
